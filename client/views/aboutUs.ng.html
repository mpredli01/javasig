About Us

The Java Users Group has been in continuous operation since February 2001 and is a forum for discussing beginner, intermediate, and advanced level Java programming. Topics will primarily focus on useful/practical applets and applications, frameworks, object-oriented programming, and software design issues.

Source code reviews will be from Java-related APIs (Spring, Groovy, JAF, JSF, etc.) developed by our own members or from outside IT professionals. We have evolved over the years to include reviews of JavaScript-related topics such as Meteor, AngularJS, and Rust.

All meetings are held every second Tuesday of the month at the Scotch Plains Rescue Squad second floor meeting hall starting promptly at 7:30pm.

About Mike

Mike has been facilitating the ACGNJ Java Users Group since its inception in February 2001. He currently also serves on the ACGNJ Board of Directors as President. Mike is a Senior Research Technician at a petrochemical research organization in Clinton, New Jersey. Mike has co-authored numerous articles with Barry and has presented at numerous venues including the annual Trenton Computer Festival and the annual Emerging Technologies for the Enterprise Conference. For more detailed information about Mike, please visit his about.me page and his personal web site, redlich.net.

About Barry

Barry has been a regular attendee and frequent contributor of the ACGNJ Java Users Group since May 2003. He is a Professor of Math and Computer Science at Drew University in Madison, New Jersey. Barry has written numerous books (including Java for Dummies and Android Application Development All-in-One For Dummies) and has presented at numerous venues including the annual Trenton Computer Festival and the annual Droidcon NYC Conference. For more detailed information about Barry, please visit his personal web site, burd.org.
